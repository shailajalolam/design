I'm A Lemon Rice!

Lemon rice is a delicious south Indian dish. Lemon juice gives a very refreshing and tangy flavor to the rice.

Ingredients used in Lemon Rice:

• 1.5 cups rice or left over rice

• salt as needed, 1 large sprig of curry leaves

• 1 or 2 tbsp. cooking oil, 1 tbsp mustard and urad dal

• 1 half tsp chana dal and 2 to 4 tbsp. peanuts or cashews

• Half tbsp turmeric powder and 1 to 2 dried red chilies broken

1 to 2 green chili slit and pinch of hing

Preparation Method:

Lemon rice can be done with fresh cooked rice or with left over rice.

If you are using uncooked rice then start by washing and soaking rice and dal, separately for 15 minutes. Once soaked, wash, drain the water and keep aside. Drain the rice and put it into the deep pan. Add water, oil, and salt, and bring to a boil. Once the water is boiling reduce the heat to low and cover the pan.

Cook about 15 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the water has evaporated. Turn off the heat and fluff the rice with a fork. Keep the cooked rice aside.

In a small bowl, mix lemon juice with turmeric powder and salt and keep aside.

Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds after seeds crack add red chili, curry leaves, and green chilies, and stir for a few seconds, and Add the chana dal, Peanuts or Cashews. and lemon juice mixture. Simmer for 2/3 mints. Stirring occasionally. Mix it well.

Now, It’s time to add the cooked rice and sprinkle salt.

Mix gently for about 1 minute and your lemon rice is ready to serve!!

Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and Serve with a Raita/Pickle and Poppadums.

I'm A Pulihora Rice!

Pulihora is usually cooked on special occasions and festive days. It is also given as prasadam in some temples; devotees queue to get Pulihora after darshana.

Ingredients used in Pulihora Rice:

• Raw Rice - 1 cup

• Tamarind - lemon sized ball

• Mustard seeds - 1 tsp

• Turmeric powder - 1/4 tsp

• Urad dal - 1 tbsp, Bengal gram/channa dal - 1 tbsp

• Sesame seed Oil for mixing with cooked rice - 1 tbsp(optional)

• Peanuts - 2 tbsp, Generous pinch of Hing(optional)

• Green chillies - 2/3 (break it into 2 pieces)

• Red chillies - 6 (break it into 2 pieces)

• Ginger (finely chopped) - 2 tsp

• 2 sprig of Curry leaves, Roasted sesame seeds powder(optional)

Preparation Method:

Cook rice in such a way that the grains are separate. Spread cooked rice on a plate, add crushed curry leaves to it when it is hot for flavor. Leave it to cool.(Do not make the rice mushy)!

Soak tamarind in hot water for 20-30 minutes and extract 1 cup of thick tamarind juice. Discard the pulp.

Heat the pan with oil, add mustard seeds, when it splutters, add urad dal, bengal gram and peanuts. Saute till dal changes to light golden brown. Then add green chillies, finely chopped ginger and saute for a few seconds.

Then add red chillies, curry leaves and fry for a few seconds. Now add tamarind extract, needed salt, hing and keep it on medium flame. Cook till the mixture thickens otherwise pulihora will be very moist.

Off the heat, add sesame seeds owder to the rice, give a quick stir do not let the mixture fry in the pan. Ass the seasoning to the rice. Mix well, Do not mix while the rice is hot.

Andhra pulihora is ready to be served with papads/appalam or fresh homemade curd.

Note:The tamarind paste or pulikachal can be stored in a bottle and refrigerated for a week. Mix it with rice when needed.

I'm A Tomato Rice!

Tomato rice is a very delicious, simple, easy and flavourful south indian recipe. This can be maded in a pressure cooker or a pot.

Ingredients used in Tomato Rice:

• 1 cup regular or basmati rice(water as required)

• 3 medium to large tomatoes, 1 medium onion

• 1.5 teaspoon ginger garlic paste and 1 green chili

• Half teaspoon mustard seeds, 4 to 5 methi seeds

• Urad dal - 1 tbsp, Bengal gram/channa dal - 1 tbsp

• Half inch cinnamon, 2 green cardamoms

• 2 to 3 cloves, 6 to 7 curry leaves

• Half teaspoon turmeric powder and red chili powder

• Half teaspoon pav bahji masala and black pepper powder

• Half teaspoon coriander powder and cumin powder

• 2 sprig of Curry leaves, One cup chopped coriander leaves

• 1.75 cups water, 2 tablespoon oil/ghee and salt as required

Preparation Method:

Heat a pressure cooker or a pot with ghee or oil.

Saute cloves, cinnamon, cumin, curry leaves and bay leaf until they begin to crackle.

Add the onions and fry them until golden and Saute the ginger paste now just for few seconds.

Add in the tomatoes, salt and turmeric. Saute for 2 to 3 mins.

Cook covered until soft and Add red chili powder, pav bhaji masala and garam masala powder.

Mix and cook until the mixture turns completely soft and mushy and Pour water according to the rice.

Stir and check salt. If needed add more. Bring the water to a rolling boil, add the drained rice.

If cooking in a pressure cooker : Stir and cover the cooker with lid. Cook on a mdeium heat. Allow to whistle once. When the pressure goes down, open the lid. Fluff up the rice.

If making in pot: Cook until the water has almost been absorbed. When the rice is till soggy, lower the flame completely and cover with a lid. Allow to cook completely. If needed sprinkle some warm water and stir. Cook until the tomato rice is completely cooked.

Add chopped coriander leaves and Tomato rice is ready to serve hot or warm. Serve tomato rice with appalams or chips or with a side raita.

Open the cap and mix that from down and Garnish chicken biryani with fresh chopped coriander leaves and Serve with raita. Enjoy the Chiken Dum Biryan!

I'm A Mutton Dum Biryani!

Hyderabadi mutton biryani is a traditional style of making the biryani by cooking the raw meat with spices for couple of minutes and then covered with rice and kept on dum until done.

Ingredients used in Mutton Dum Biryani:

• 1 kg mutton cleaned and washed well and Basmati rice - 5cups fo rice(600gms)

• Turmeric powder - 1tsp, Saffron - 1tsp.

• Coriander leaves and Mint - 1bunch.

• Salt - 1 to taste, Oil/ghee - 5tbsp

• Red chilli powder - 1tbsp and Food colors(optional)

• Boiled egg for decoration (optional) - 2

• Rose water (optional) - 1tbsp.

• Garlic and ginger paste - 2tbsp.

• Golden fried sliced onions - 2cup.

• 1 cinnamon stick, 3-4 green cardamoms

• 4-5 black pepper corns, 2-3 cloves

• Green chilli - 3(paste) and Curd - 2 cup.

• Cashew nuts (optional) - 50 grams.

• Black cumin - 1 tsp,1 tsp raw papaya paste

• Garam masala powder - 2tbsp and • Lime juice - 2tbsp.

• 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves for garnishing

• 1 tbsp chopped mint leaves for garnishing

Preparation Method:

Firstly, in a large vessel, add washed mutton pieces, salt, red chilli powder, turmeric powder, ginger garlic paste.

Into the marinade add cinnamon stick, cardamoms, cloves, black pepper corns, and mix well. Add green chilli paste, mint leaves, coriander leaves, crush the deep fried onions and add into it. Mix the whole mixture well. Add raw papaya paste, add oil, add beaten yogurt into the marinade, add garam masala powder, add lemon juice and mix it well. Rest this marinade aside for about 1 hour.

Wash basmati rice and soak the rice in water for about 30/40 minutes. In a cooking pot, add water and salt generously for about 10-12 cups or more than that.

Add whole spices that are cinnamon, cardamoms, caraway seeds, cloves and black pepper corns and ghee into it and mix it well. Bring the water a boil. Add the soaked rice into the boiling water. Cook the rice until it is 60-70% done. Strain the rice completely and put it aside.

Take a cooking pot in which Biryani is cooked. Add the mutton marinade into it and spread it evenly. Add the rice over the marinade and spread it evenly.

Over the rice season it with saffron flavored milk, Rose water, mint leaves, corinader leaves. Add lemon juice all over and add crushed onions all over the rice. Top it with some desi ghee.

Cover the lid and seal it tightly with a dough or put heavy weight on the lid. Switch on the flame. Place a skillet/tawa on the flame. Place the cooking pot on the tawa. Dum the biryani for about 15 minutes on high flame. Reduce the flame to sim and dum the biryani for about 35/40 mints on sim flame. Switch off the flame. Dig the biryani from one side of the corner. The biryani should be taken out with a spatula from the bottom to the top. Serve the mutton dum biryani with raita and mirchi ka salan.

I'm A Shrimp Curry!

Andhra Style Spicy Prawn Curry is a spicy and tasty prawn curry. This curry as side dish for chappathi or roti.

Ingredients used in Shrimp Curry:

• 400 grams of Shrimp/Royyalu

• Onion - 2 (medium sized - chopped finely)

• Tomato - 2 (chop finely and make into puree)

• Half tsp Coriander powder and Cumin powder

• 1 green chili slit and 1 tsp. ginger garlic paste

• 1 sprig curry leaves, 1tsp jeera

• Salt to taste and 2tbsp. oil(adjust to suit your taste)

• 1 tsp. garam masala powder, 1tsp Kashmiri chilli powder(optional)

• 1tsp turmeric and 1tbsp Red chilli powder

• 1 tbsp. Lemon juice (adjust to suit your taste)

• Fennel seeds - 1/2 tsp, Cardamom - 1, Cloves - 6 to 7

• Bay leaf - 1 crushed, Cinnamon stick - 2 inch (Crushed)

• Coriander leaves few for garnishing

Preparation Method:

Clean and devein the prawns. Wash with salt. Drain water thoroughly, Marinate the prawns with salt, chilli powder, coriander powder, turmeric and lemon juice. Let it sit for 30 - 40 mints atleast.

Heat a pan with add oil, and when hot add the spices one by one and Add the chopped onions, curry leaves, green chillies. Sauté till they become soft. Add the Ginger garlic paste. Fry till they raw smell goes off.

Now add the tomato purée, remaining chilli powder, kashmiri chilli, coriander powder, salt, little turmeric. add the cumin powder.

Saute them nicely. Take care not to burn them. Add the marinated prawns and cook for while. Sprinkle some water just enough to bring in some moisture for the prawns to get cooked. Cover and cook on a medium flame till they are completely cooked. Cook till all water goes off. Switch off the stove and served hot.

Spicy and yummy, Andhra style prawn curry is ready, Garnish with coriander leaves.

Notes: Taste before adding garam masala and pepper powder, since it becomes more spicy and kids cannot eat it.

I'm A Chapala Pulusu!

"Chepala" means River fish or Fresh water fish. An awesome (pulusu) dish made with fish and tangy tamarind sauce is the traditional delicacy of Andhra Pradesh. Chepala Pulusu means fish curry in tamarind sauce.

Ingredients used in Chapala Pulusu:

• 1 kg fish - cut into medium size pieces

• 1 Onion(chopped) and 1 Tomato(chopped)

• Green chilli slit – 3, Ginger garlic paste – 2 tbsp + 1 tbsp for marination

• 1 tbsp Mustard seeds and Cumin

• Curry Leaves - 1spring, Fenugreek seeds – 1/4 tbsp

• 2 tbsp of Red Chili Powder (I used Kashmiri Chili Powder)

• Cumin and Coriander powder – 2 tbsp

• Turmeric and Pepper powder – 1 tbsp

• Salt to taste and Oil – 6-7 tbsp

• Tamarind soaked in hot water – 1 lemon size

• 1 bunch of coriander chopped finely

Preparation Method:

Soak tamarind in hot water for atleast 20 min and extract juice out of it. Discard the pulp.Now start marinating the fish pieces with spice powders. Make the spice powders into 2 halves and sprinkle one half on the fish pieces along with 1/2 tbsp of ginger garlic paste.Let them marinate for at-least 30 - 40 mints.

Take oil into a heavy bottomed pan/pot and add oil, crackle cumin seeds,mustard seeds and curry leaves. Then add onions,green chilli and saute for a min. Add in ginger garlic paste and saute till the onions are soft.

Add in tomatoes and cook till they turn mushy.Adjust some salt and sprinkle the remaining spice powders.

Add in the extracted tamarind juice and adjust some water(use less) and boil them on high flame for 5/7mints.

Add in the marinated fish pieces and boil them on high flame for another 5/7mints. Reduce the flame and cook it covered for atleast 20 min or until the fish is cooked inside.Adjust salt if required

Sprinkle lots of coriander and serve hot!!Tangy fish pulusu is ready to enjoy:)

Note: Usage of grated fresh coconut gives it a total different taste so feel free to use.

Allow the fish curry to sleep for a night..it gives a better taste.

I'm A Kodi Pulusu!

Andhra cuisine is popular for hot and spicy foods and especially the foods made in vijaywada and Guntur region.

Ingredients used in Kodi Pulusu:

• Half kg chicken, ¼ tbsp. red chili powder (adjust as needed)

• medium onions finely chopped

• 1 tomato chopped finely, 1 green chili slit or chopped

• ¾ tsp ginger garlic paste, 3 tbsp. Oil

•3 tbsp. Poppy seeds or 10 cashewnuts, 2 tbsp. Coconut (optional)

• 1tbsp. ginger garlic paste and Kashmiri chilli powder

• ⅛ tsp turmeric, Salt as needed

• 1 to 3 tbsp. lemon juice (adjust as needed) or curd

• 2 tbsp. red chili powder (adjust as needed)

• 1 bay leaf or 1 sprig curry leaves

• 2 to 3 cloves, 2 cardamom, 1 inch cinnamon stick

• ¼ shahi jeera or cumin, 1 Garam masala powder

• 1 bunch coriander leaves chopped

Preparation Method:

Sun dry or dry roast poppy seeds. First powder them thoroughly to a fine powder and then add water to make a smooth paste.Use very little water. If you cannot make a smooth paste, do not attempt to use the poppy paste, instead replace with 10 cashew nuts paste.If using coconut add it along with water to make a fine paste or thick milk like consistency.

Marinate chicken with marination ingredients and set aside till we use it. Add oil in a pan and heat till hot, sauté the whole spices in the oil for a min. Add green chili, salt and onions. Fry till onions become fragrant and golden evenly. Add ginger garlic paste and saute again till it becomes fragrant.

Please do not burn. If using tomato, you can add now and fry till the mixture is roasted well and oil begins to separate.

Add chicken and fry on a high flame for 2 to 3 mints, add Red chili powder,Kashmiri chilli and garam masala, add water just enough to immerse the chicken.Do not add lot of water. Curry becomes runny.

Cover and cook on a low flame till the chicken is fully cooked and is tender. Add the poppy seeds/cashew paste. Stir well and cook till you get the required consistency for about 3 to 5 minutes.

Serve andhra chicken curry with any jeera rice or phulka/roti and raita.

I'm A Tomato Chutney!

Tomato chutney is a south indian curry it is easy to make and it is very simple Chutney. Chutneys are freshly made from fresh ingredients that can be kept a couple of days or a week in the refrigerator. Chutneys are commonly made in every indian home.

Ingredients used in Tomato Chutney:

• 2 large tomatoes, ½ inch ginger and 1 small garlic pod

• 2 to 3 dry red chilies, 1 teaspoon urad dal

• 4­5 black pepper, 2­3 cloves, 1 pinch asafoetida

• 2 tablespoon water for grinding, ½ tablespoon oil, salt as required

For Tempering:

• ½ tablespoon oil, 1 dry red chili

• 3 springs curry leaves, ½ teaspoon mustard seeds

• 2 to 3 fenugreek seeds, 1 pinch hing

Preparation Method:

Heat oil and add urad dal. on a low flame, saute the urad dal. Dal must turn golden.

Once the lentils get a golden brown color, add broken dry red chilies, cloves, black pepper and ginger, garlic stir for till the red chilies change their color. Don not burn.

Then add chopped tomatoes and asafoetida. add salt. stir and saute till the tomatoes soften. about 6 to 7 minutes on a low flame. Once the tomato mixture cools, add them to a chutney grinder or small blender.

Add 2 tbsp water and grind to a smooth paste. In the same pan or different pan, heat 1 tbsp oil. Add the mustard seeds and crackle them. then add curry leaves, methi seeds, asafoetida, and one broken red chili. saute till the curry leaves become crisp.

Then add the ground tomato paste. stir well. Saute for 3 to 4 minutes on a low flame. check the taste and add more salt if required.

Stir again. the tomato chutney is done and ready to be served.

Cool completely and remove to a serving vessel/Glass jar. Serve with any tiffin like idli and pongal OR with rice OR as a dip

I'm A Onion - Tomato Chutney!

Onion -Tomato chutney recipe to accompany south indian breakfasts like Idli, Dosa, Pesarattu, Pongal.

Ingredients used in Onion - Tomato Chutney:

• 2 medium onions, 3 tomatoes(adjust to suit your taste)

• 3 to 5 red chilies, 1tbsp chana dal

• Half tbsp urad dal, Half inch ginger, 1 garlic clove

• Half tsp jeera, Salt as needed, 2 tsp oil

• Tamarind paste, Pinch of turmeric

For Tempering:

• 1 tsp.oil, ¼ tsp urad dal

• pinch of hing, 1 sprig curry leaves

• ¼ tsp mustard, ¼ tsp cumin, 1 broken red chili

Preparation Method:

Dry roast dals till golden.Heat the oil. Fry red chilies till crisp. set aside. Add the onions and saute till light brown. add the tomatoes and asafoetida. season with salt.

Saute till the tomatoes become soft, pulpy and are completely cooked. the rawness of the tomatoes should go away, otherwise you will get the raw aroma of the tomatoes in the chutney fry till the tomatoes turn mushy and soft. Cool this as well.

Add dal, cumin and red chilies to a blender. Make a fine powder. Add onion tomato mixture, ginger garlic and then grind in a chutney grinder with tamarind. no need to add water while grinding. but if you are unable to grind then add little water.

Check the taste and add some more salt or tamarind if required and continue to grind to a smooth chutney.

Heat oil in the pan, add cumin, mustard, urad dal. When the dal turns golden, add curry leaves and red chilies. Fry till the leaves turn crisp. add hing. Add onion tomato chutney to the pan and mix it well.

Pour the chutney in a serving bowl.Serve with Idli, Dosa, Medu Vada and Pesarratu.

• ½ lime size tamarind, ½ lime size jaggery(optional)

For the Tempering:

• 2 teaspoon Oil, 2 dried Red Chilli

• 1 sprig Curry leaves, 1 tsp musterd and cumin

• Pinch of Turmeric(optional)

Preparation Method:

Heat oil in a pan and fry the peanuts on a low to medium flame for 3­4 minutes.

Add the curry leaves and fry for a minute. lastly add the chana dal, sesame seeds, methii seeds & asafoetida and fry for 2­3 minutes more on a low flame. Take care that you don't over brown or burn this mixture, else the chutney might have bitter tones.

Let this mixture warm or cool down. Then, addin a chutney grinder or a small blender. Add in the ginger, garlic, green chilies, tamarind and salt.

Pour some water and grind the peanut chutney to a smooth consistency. You can make the peanut chutney thick or of medium consistency. Check the salt and add more if required.

You can serve as is or do a simple tadka/tempering the following way.

Heat oil in a pan and add couple of dried red chillies and curry leaves. When the curry leaves turn crisp (usually 30-40 seconds in medium flame), remove off heat and add it to the chutney.

Serve peanut chutney with idli, dosa, medu vada, uttapam or pakoras.

I'm A Double ka Meetha!

Double ka meetha is a dessert of Hyderabad, Telangana. It is popular in Hyderabadi cuisine, served at weddings and parties.

Ingredients used in Double ka Meetha:

• 10 slices of fresh bread (brown/white)

• 1 cup water, 1 cup sugar, 2 tablespoons rose water

• Safron color - 1 pinch, Cardamom powder - 1tsp

• 1 litre milk, Khoya - 200g, Ghee - 2cups

• 10-12 almonds, 10-12 Cashew nuts, 2 tablespoons Raisins,Pista,Dated

Preparation Method:

Heat ghee in a frying pan. Cut the bread into halves diagonally. Deep fry the slices till golden brown. Drain on a tissue lined plate and Keep aside.

Deep fry the almonds and cashew nuts in the same ghee till slightly browned and Drain and keep aside.

For the Sugar Syrup: Add sugar and water in a pan and cook on medium heat till the syrup is slightly thickened.Remove from heat and keep aside.

For the Milk Mixture: Heat milk in a heavy bottom pan.When the milk comes to a boil, lower the heat and add crumbled khoya in it. Cook on low heat till the milk is reduced to half. Once the milk turns into a thick mixture, add a pinch of saffron and mix well. Remove from heat and keep aside. Add rose water.

Dip the toasts in the sugar syrup and place them in a serving plate. Pour the milk mixture over the bread slices and garnish with toasted pistas.

Serve Shahi Tukra/Double ka Meetha warm or chilled.

I'm A Bread Halwa!

Bread halwa is very delicious sweet. Bread halwa is one of the easiest Indian halwa recipes. Bread halwa can be made during festivals like diwali, pongal as it has very simple and quick process.

Ingredients used in Bread Halwa:

• Bread – 7 slices, Ghee – 1/2 cup)

• Milk – 2 cups, Sugar – 1/2 cup + 2 tbsp

• Almond – 8, Cashew nuts – 10, Cardamom – 3

Preparation Method:

Cut the bread into bit sized pieces. Heat Kadai or a heavy bottomed pan with ghee.

And add the bread pieces and fry them till they turn a golden brown. Taking care not to turn the pieces dark in color. It should be golden in colour.

Meanwhile, boil milk with sugar. Add it to the roasted bread.

Bring to boil and put the flame to low.. Let it simmer till the bread pieces turn soft and cook in the milk mixture. Garnish with the almonds and cashew nuts.

Serve warm or refrigerate until chilled and serve. It tastes good both warm and cold.

Note: This should keep good 3 days in the fridge.

I'm A Rice Kheer!

Rice kheer can be made for any festival or celebration or also as a sweet dessert wihich you can serve after meals. Making kheer at home is very easy.

Ingredients used in Rice Kheer:

• 1 litre Whole milk, 5 to 6 tbsp sugar

• ¼ cup Long grain rice or Basmati rice

• Rose water- 1tsp(optional), 6 green Cardamoms, 1 pinch Saffron, 1 tbsp Raisins

• 15 to 20 Almonds and 15 to 20 Cashews(chopped)

Preparation Method:

Wash the rice under running cold water till water runs clear. Soak the rice in enough water for 30 minutes.

While rice is soaking, chop the nuts and keep it aside. After soaking time, discard the water.

Take milk in a heavy bottom pan/pot. Turn the heat on medium.

Let the milk come to a boil. Once it starts boiling, add rice.

Stir and simmer the milk and let the rice grains cook.

The whole process of cooking the rice in the milk takes about approx 35­40mints on a low flame.

Now add sugar and cardamom powder. Stir it well. Stir and continue to stir often so that lumps are not formed and scrape the sides of the pan and add this dried milk into the pan.

Also add saffron strands and chopped cashews, almonds and raisins.

Mix well. let it simmer for 6­7 minutes. It should be thick now.

How to check right consistency: Take ladleful of kheer and pour it back. Rice and Milk should fall together in the same flow. They should not be separated like earlier. Once you get this consistency then turn off the stove.

You can serve the rice kheer, hot or warm. or pour in serving bowls and refrigerate.

This rice kheer stays good for 1­2 days in the refrigerator.

Notes:Rose water can also be added to the rice kheer.
